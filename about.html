About

I am a Postdoctoral Fellow at the Steve & Cindy Rasmussen Institute for Genomic Medicine, Nationwide Children's Hospital, Columbus, OH, working with Prof. Jayajit Das.

My work combines theoretical and computational approaches to study biological systems, with a current focus on modeling Natural Killer (NK) cell responses and CAR-NK cell therapies. I develop multiscale in silico models that integrate stochastic protein–protein interaction networks, cell-growth dynamics, and experimental data to bridge molecular mechanisms and cellular-scale behavior.

Before joining Nationwide Children's Hospital, I was a postdoctoral fellow at the University of California, Riverside. I completed my Ph.D. in Physics at the Indian Institute of Technology (IIT) Bombay, where I studied first-passage problems in stochastic systems with resetting under the supervision of Prof. Dibyendu Das.

Research Interests

My current research interests lie at the interface of computational biology, statistical physics, and immunology. I am particularly interested in stochastic dynamics of immune cells, spatial statistics of receptor organization, and multiscale modeling of therapeutic cell responses.
