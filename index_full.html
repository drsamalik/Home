About

I am a postdoctoral fellow at the Steve & Cindy Rasmussen Institute for Genomic Medicine, Nationwide Children's Hospital, working with Prof. Jayajit Das. My work combines theoretical and computational approaches to study biological systems, with a current focus on modeling Natural Killer (NK) cell responses and CAR-NK cell therapies. Before joining Nationwide Children's Hospital, I was a postdoctoral fellow at the University of California, Riverside, and completed my Ph.D. in Physics at IIT Bombay.

Research

Immunology & NK cell modeling (current focus)

I develop multiscale computational models of Natural Killer (NK) cell responses to predict CAR-NK cytotoxic activity across target cell types, to explore clonal variability in early NK cell responses, and to analyze spatial statistics of receptor clustering that shape early signal discrimination. These models integrate stochastic protein–protein interaction networks, cell-growth dynamics, and experimental data to bridge molecular mechanisms and cellular-scale behavior.

Stochastic processes and first-passage problems

My earlier and ongoing theoretical work investigates first-passage problems in stochastic systems, in particular processes with stochastic resetting. I have studied how resetting, spatial dimension, attractive bias, potential barriers, and time overheads shape optimal target-search strategies, identifying parameter regimes where the optimal resetting rate exhibits vanishing transitions.

Virus capsid assembly

I have also studied the assembly and disassembly of virus capsids, examining how these processes unfold under different physiological conditions and what determines the fate of the enclosed viral genome.
